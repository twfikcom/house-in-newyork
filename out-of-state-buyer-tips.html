Navigating the New York Real Estate Market: Tips for Out-of-State Buyers

Buying in New York is unlike any other state. The prevalence of Co-ops, the necessity of real estate attorneys, and the "Mansion Tax" catch many off-guard.

For out-of-state buyers, understanding the distinction between a Condo and a Co-op is step one. Step two is assembling a local "dream team" of brokers and lawyers who specialize in NYC-specific closing procedures.

The Attorney Factor

In New York State, real estate attorneys are mandatory for the closing process. Unlike states where escrow companies handle the bulk of the paperwork, NYC requires legal counsel to review the offering plan (for condos/co-ops) and draft the contract of sale.
