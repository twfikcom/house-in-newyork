Closing Checklist for NYC Home Sales

A definitive step-by-step guide to navigating the final 60-day gauntlet of the New York purchase process.

"In New York, the closing is not an event; it is a legal marathon. Failure to prepare for the final 30 days is the primary reason deals collapse."

The 60-Day Timeline

From the moment your offer is "Accepted," the clock starts. In the 2026 market, efficiency is the only way to prevent "Seller Fatigue" or being gazumped by a cash offer.

Phase 1: The Contract (Days 1-7)

Your attorney must review the **Offering Plan** and the building's financial statements immediately. In New York, "Time is of the Essence" is a legal term that is rarely used but always implied. You must sign the contract and provide the 10% deposit (held in your attorney’s escrow account) within this first week.

Phase 2: The Mortgage & Title (Days 10-30)

Simultaneously, your lender will issue a "Mortgage Commitment Letter." This is the only document that truly secures the deal. Your attorney will also order a **Title Search** or a **Lien Search** (for co-ops) to ensure the seller has the legal right to transfer ownership without any "clouds" on the title.

The Board Package Mastery

If buying a Co-op, this is the most critical phase. You must provide two years of full tax returns, letters of reference, and a net-worth statement. In 2026, boards are looking for **24 months of liquid assets** remaining AFTER you have paid the down payment and closing costs.

Phase 3: The Final Walkthrough (Day 59)

Never skip this. You must ensure the property is in "Broom Clean" condition and that all appliances are working as they were during the inspection. In New York, once you sign the final documents, you "accept" the property’s condition as-is.

The Closing Table

The physical closing in 2026 often happens at the seller’s attorney's office or the lender's office. You will sign dozens of documents, including the **HUD-1 Settlement Statement**, which itemizes every dollar spent. Bring several copies of your ID and be prepared for a 3-hour session.

Conclusion

The NYC closing process is designed to protect all parties, but it requires extreme organization. By following this 60-day blueprint, you ensure that your move into your new New York home is a celebration, not a legal crisis.
